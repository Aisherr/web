Educational Tips

Oily Skin

Oily skin is a type of skin that produces more sebum than normal. Sebum is a waxy, oily substance that lubricates and protects the skin. Oily skin can have a greasy appearance, large pores, and a higher risk of acne. Oily skin can be caused by various factors, such as hormones, genetics, stress, environment, and lifestyle.

Dry Skin

Dry skin is a condition in which low water levels or oil in the skin can create a tight, uncomfortable feeling, among other symptoms. Dry skin is common, though it can sometimes be a sign of more significant health issues.

Combination Skin

Combination skin is a skin type where some areas of the face are oily and others are dry or normal. The oily areas are usually the T-zone, which covers the forehead, nose, and chin. The dry areas are often the cheeks, jaw, and hairline. This is related to the number and rate of oil-producing glands on each part of the face.

Sensitive Skin

Sensitivity is a term that describes skin that reacts easily to various factors, such as cosmetics, personal care products, environmental elements, hormones, or skin treatments. Sensitive skin may have symptoms like burning, itching, or dryness. Sensitive skin can be a natural condition or a result of a weakened or damaged skin barrier.
